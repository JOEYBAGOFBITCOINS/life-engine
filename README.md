Life Engine | Digital Twin Simulation

A lightweight digital twin simulation exploring how lifestyle, stress, and repair mechanisms affect biological aging over time.

This project was built in GitHub Codespaces while I was supposed to be working on other homework and building a business. Instead, curiosity took over and a small longevity simulation engine emerged.
Sometimes my A.D.D. is a super power...

Overview

Life Engine models a simplified representation of human biological systems and visualizes how different interventions affect aging.

The simulation tracks:

- Chronological age
- Biological age
- Vitality
- Repair capacity
- Inflammation
- Cellular damage
- Stress load

It also models several body systems:

- Brain
- Heart
- Metabolism
- Immune system
- Mobility
- Recovery

The goal is to simulate how repair vs decay forces interact over time.

If repair exceeds damage long enough, the system approaches what longevity researchers call Longevity Escape Velocity.


Features

Life Engine currently includes:

- Digital twin body visualization
- Longevity reactor core showing escape velocity
- Autonomous life simulation
- Lifestyle interventions (sleep, exercise, nutrition, etc.)
- Biological vs chronological aging timeline
- System health modeling across major body systems
- Event log tracking biological changes
- Fast-forward simulation controls

The system can simulate aging trajectories and show how lifestyle and repair mechanisms affect long-term biological outcomes.

Interventions

The simulation allows different lifestyle and biological interventions.

Examples include:

- Sleep
- Exercise
- Nutrition
- Meditation
- Stress events
- Repair therapy
- Advance time by 30 days

Each intervention affects multiple biological variables.

For example:

Exercise improves

- vitality
- heart health
- metabolism
- mobility

Stress increases

- inflammation
- stress load
- biological aging

Autonomous Simulation

The digital twin can run autonomously, simulating months of biological activity.

The system introduces random events such as:

- exercise improvements
- recovery through sleep
- stress events
- nutrition improvements
- repair therapies
- baseline aging

This allows the model to explore how complex interactions between repair and damage processes influence long-term biological aging.

Aging Timeline

A chart visualizes the relationship between:

- Chronological age
- Biological age

Healthy behaviors may slow or reverse biological aging trends, while stress and accumulated damage accelerate them.

Escape Velocity Concept

The simulation calculates a simplified representation of longevity escape velocity.

Repair Power = repair + vitality + system health

Decay Force = damage + inflammation + stress

Escape Velocity = Repair Power − Decay Force


When repair processes consistently outpace biological decay, the system approaches a state where aging slows dramatically or potentially reverses.

Why I Built This

I’m currently studying computer science while also building a startup.

But the real reason I built this is simple:

Because this is the future.

Medicine is moving toward a world where every person has a digital twin - a living model of their biology that continuously tracks health, predicts risks, and simulates interventions before they happen in the real world.

Instead of relying on trial-and-error experiments or testing on our beloved furry friends, digital simulations could allow researchers to model outcomes safely and ethically.

AI and simulation technology have the potential to dramatically accelerate medical discovery while reducing harm.

That future may sound ambitious, but this project explores a small step toward it.

Instead of just talking about that future, I wanted to start building a small working version of the idea.

Life Engine is a first step toward exploring what a personal longevity simulation system might look like.
